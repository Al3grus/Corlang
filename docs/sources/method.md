# The Corlang method, what the app's daily structure is built on

- **Sources:** Dunlosky, Rawson, Marsh, Nathan & Willingham (2013), *Improving Students'
  Learning With Effective Learning Techniques*, Psychological Science in the Public Interest
  14(1), 242 studies; re-confirmed by Donoghue & Hattie (2021) meta-analysis (Frontiers in
  Education). CEFR action-oriented approach (Council of Europe). ASOO curriculum hours
  (`asoo-curriculum.md`). Croaticum unit format (`croaticum-syllabus.md`). Fetched/verified
  2026-07-10.

## What the evidence says

1. **Practice testing (retrieval) and distributed practice are the two highest-utility
   techniques known**, robust across ages, materials and outcome measures (Dunlosky 2013:
   the only two rated "high utility"; rereading and highlighting rate low). The app therefore
   *leads every session with retrieval* (SRS flashcards, quizzes, drills) and *spaces
   everything* (Leitner scheduler, daily sessions, weekly consolidation days).
2. **Spacing beats massing**, many shorter distributed sessions outperform rare long ones,
   including specifically for vocabulary learning. Hence daily sessions and gym micro-sets
   rather than weekend cramming; a streak system to protect the distribution.
3. **Interleaving** (mixing topics) has moderate-positive evidence, the SRS queue naturally
   interleaves packs and levels; quizzes mix grammar points.
4. **Comprehensible input + pushed output** (CEFR's action-oriented model, used by Croaticum):
   learners need daily input slightly above their level AND regular forced production.
   Input = the official e-course units (audio-first); output = writing tasks and conversation
   with a native partner, scheduled by the plan.
5. **Testing at scale**: milestone mock exams (days 180, 225, 235) exploit the testing effect
   on whole-exam performance and calibrate readiness against the official 60% rule.

## How long (official numbers)

The Croatian state curriculum (ASOO) allocates **140 h (A1) + 140 h (A2) + 280 h (B1) = 560
teaching hours** to reach B1. The 240-day plan distributes this: ~45 min e-course/textbook
input + ~15-20 min app work per day ≈ 60-75 min/day, splittable into micro-sessions. Scope
decision: **the app targets A1 → B1** (the certificate exam); B2 exists as reference content
only until B1 is passed.

## The daily session shape (the player's five phases)

| # | Phase | Science | In the app |
|---|-------|---------|-----------|
| 1 | **Recall** | practice testing, spacing | due SRS words first, every day |
| 2 | **Input** | comprehensible input | today's e-course unit / textbook chapter (official only) |
| 3 | **Practice** | testing effect, interleaving | in-app drills (gender drill, quizzes, grammar tab) |
| 4 | **Output** | pushed output | speak/write tasks, partner conversation, exam-genre writing |
| 5 | **Wrap-up** | spacing, review | the 15-minute closing review of the day |

## Resource policy (user-set, 2026-07-10)

Official exam-path resources ONLY: the two University of Zagreb e-courses, Croaticum's exam
page, the official Croaticum textbook (B1 phase), HRT news (kept strictly because the official
exam's listening section IS news-bulletin format, see `croaticum-b1-sample.md`), and
conversation with a native partner. No blogs, no community forums, no third-party channels.
