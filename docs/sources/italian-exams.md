# Italian exams: CILS (Siena) and CELI (Perugia), A1 to B1

**Verified live: 2026-07-20.** Exam formats and citizenship law change; re-verify before authoring
any further Italian level or before launch.

Sources: the Università per Stranieri di Siena CILS centre (`cils.unistrasi.it`) and its published
*Criteri di attribuzione dei punteggi*; the Università per Stranieri di Perugia CVCL
(`unistrapg.it`, `cvcl.unistrapg.it`) and its published *Criteri di valutazione e punteggi* and
*Descrizione esame* documents; the Associazione CLIQ; the Ministero dell'Interno; and the
Constitutional Court.

Corlang references these formats; it never reproduces exam material. Mock exams mirror the
STRUCTURE and pass rules only, with all texts, items and prompts written fresh.

## Why learners sit these exams

Italian citizenship by residence (art. 9, L. 91/1992) and by marriage or civil union (art. 5)
requires Italian at **B1**. The requirement was introduced by art. 9.1 of L. 91/1992, inserted by
D.L. 4 October 2018 n. 113 as converted by **L. 1 December 2018 n. 132**, in force from
**4 December 2018**. Confirmed still current on 2026-07-20. This is the legal driver that puts B1
at the end of the Corlang Italian course.

The separate EU long-term residence permit (permesso di soggiorno UE per soggiornanti di lungo
periodo) requires only **A2**, under the Ministero dell'Interno decree of 4 June 2010. Two
different bars, two different levels, and learners routinely confuse them.

**Accepted certifications.** The Ministero dell'Interno accepts certificates from the four bodies
of the **CLIQ** system (Certificazione Lingua Italiana di Qualità), identified most recently by
D.M. 7 December 2021:

| Body | Certification |
|------|---------------|
| Università per Stranieri di Siena | CILS |
| Università per Stranieri di Perugia | CELI |
| Università degli Studi Roma Tre | cert.it |
| Società Dante Alighieri | PLIDA |

A school-leaving qualification from an Italian state or state-recognised school is also accepted in
place of a certificate.

**Correction to a common assumption:** CLIQ has **four** members, not five. **Ce.Co.L.** is a real
certification, issued by the Università per Stranieri "Dante Alighieri" di Reggio Calabria, and it
is recognised by the education and foreign ministries for university enrolment purposes, but it is
**not** a CLIQ member and it does **not** appear among the four bodies listed by the Interior
Ministry for citizenship. Corlang should not present it as a citizenship route. Verified against
the CLIQ members page and the Interior Ministry pages on 2026-07-20.

**2025 change, still in force.** Constitutional Court **sentenza n. 25 of 7 March 2025** struck
down art. 9.1 in the part where it provided no exemption for applicants who are objectively and
documentably unable to acquire the language because of disability. Applicants with serious
limitations on language learning certified by a public health authority are exempt from the B1
requirement. The B1 bar itself is unchanged for everyone else.

**2026 changes: none found.** No amendment to the B1 citizenship requirement and no change to the
list of accepted certifying bodies was found as of 2026-07-20. Italy's 2025 citizenship reform
activity concerned descent-based (jus sanguinis) claims, not the language requirement. Treat this
as "nothing found today", not as a guarantee.

## The B1 cittadinanza exam (the shortened variant)

Both Siena and Perugia publish a **shortened B1 built only for citizenship**: **CILS B1
Cittadinanza** and **CELI 2 i (B1) Cittadinanza**. They are separate exams with their own construct
and format, not a lighter sitting of the standard B1.

**The caveat that matters.** The Università per Stranieri di Siena states that B1 Cittadinanza
describes a much more limited portion of competence than the standard B1 and that its usability is
therefore **limited to citizenship purposes only**. It does **not** cover work or academic purposes.
A learner who wants a certificate for a job or a university place must sit the standard **CILS
UNO-B1** or **CELI 2**, not the citizenship variant. This distinction must be surfaced in the app;
learners buy the cheap short exam and later discover it is not accepted where they need it.

**CILS B1 Cittadinanza structure**, from the official Siena scoring criteria:

| Section | Composition | Max points |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Ascolto | 2 tests (6 multiple-choice items; 12 information-location items) | 12 |
| Comprensione della lettura e riflessione grammaticale | 2 tests (12 information-location items; 6 multiple-choice completion items) | 12 |
| Produzione scritta | 1 task, 80 to 120 words | 12 |
| Produzione orale | 1 task | 12 |

Total duration about **2h 15m**, including roughly 10 minutes of oral. Four abilities, no separate
"strutture" section (grammar is folded into the reading section).

**CELI 2 i (B1) Cittadinanza structure**, from the official CVCL exam description: components A
Comprensione della Lettura, B Produzione e interazione scritta, C Comprensione dell'Ascolto, plus a
Prova orale. Two booklets (A and B together, C separately). Total duration **2 hours 30 minutes**:
2 hours for the first booklet, 20 minutes for listening, 10 to 12 minutes oral. Each of the four
components carries **25%**. The oral includes civics questions and an intercultural comparison,
which the general CELI 2 oral does not.

## CILS (Università per Stranieri di Siena)

CILS levels map to CEFR as: **A1, A2, UNO-B1, DUE-B2, TRE-C1, QUATTRO-C2**.

Standard-level exams test **five abilities**: ascolto, comprensione della lettura, produzione
scritta, produzione orale, and capacità di gestire le strutture della comunicazione (the grammar
and language-structures section).

### Pass rule: MODULAR, per ability, with a total floor

CILS is **modular in the strict sense**: sufficiency must be reached in **every single ability**.
A total above the threshold does not rescue a failed section. The sections are never averaged.

| Exam | Abilities | Max per ability | Total max | Min per ability | Min total |
|------|-----------|-----------------|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| CILS A1 (standard) | 5 | 12 | 60 | 7 | 35 |
| CILS A2 (standard) | 5 | 12 | 60 | 7 | 35 |
| CILS UNO-B1 (standard) | 5 | 20 | 100 | 11 | 55 |
| CILS A2 Integrazione | 4 | 12 | 48 | 7 | 28 |
| CILS B1 Cittadinanza | 4 | 12 | 48 | 7 | 28 |

The two conditions are cumulative: **every ability must reach its own minimum, and the total must
reach the floor**. Because the per-ability minimum times the number of abilities equals the total
floor exactly in every row above (5 x 7 = 35, 5 x 11 = 55, 4 x 7 = 28), the per-ability rule is the
binding one. Corlang implements the per-ability rule literally and reports the total alongside it.

Approximate durations: A1 and A2 about 2 hours, B1 standard about 3 hours (the Siena-derived
breakdown gives about 3h 55m including a 10-minute oral for the full B1), B1 Cittadinanza about
2h 15m.

### Capitalizzazione (carry-over), and where it does NOT apply

For **standard** CILS levels, a candidate who passes some abilities and fails others receives an
*attestato di capitalizzazione* listing which abilities were passed. The passed abilities carry over
for **18 months**, and the candidate re-sits only the failed abilities, possibly at a different exam
centre.

**Capitalizzazione does NOT apply to CILS B1 Cittadinanza.** Fail one ability and the entire exam
must be re-sat. This is confirmed both by Siena's own materials and by Italian Cultural Institute
exam guidance. It makes the citizenship variant, despite being shorter, the harsher pass rule of
the two.

### Source conflict, resolved

An Istituto Italiano di Cultura information sheet (Algeri, PDF dated 2023) states that B1
Cittadinanza is scored out of **100 with a minimum of 55 and 11 per ability**. That is the scale for
**CILS B1 Standard**, not for the citizenship variant. The official Siena *Criteri di attribuzione
dei punteggi* for B1 Cittadinanza show four sections worth 12 points each, giving 48. The figures
**48 total, 12 per ability, 7 minimum per ability, 28 minimum total** are the ones to use. Preferring
the exam body over the third party, as the rule requires.

**Partially verified:** the per-ability minimum of 7 and the total floor of 35 for **standard A1 and
A2** could not be read directly off `cils.unistrasi.it` today, because the site's scoring pages
(`I_punteggi.htm`, `La_valutazione.htm` in some URL forms, and the B1 Cittadinanza page) returned
HTTP 500 on repeated attempts. Those two numbers come from CILS exam-centre documentation that
reproduces the official rule, and they are arithmetically consistent with the official totals
(5 abilities x 12 = 60; 5 x 7 = 35). Re-verify against Siena directly when the site is up.

## CELI (Università per Stranieri di Perugia)

### Level naming, which confuses everyone

CELI numbering does **not** match CEFR numbering. The certificate called "CELI 2" is a B1, not a B2.

| CELI name | CEFR |
|-----------|------|
| CELI Impatto | A1 |
| CELI 1 | A2 |
| CELI 2 | B1 |
| CELI 3 | B2 |
| CELI 4 | C1 |
| CELI 5 | C2 |

Variants exist alongside the general exams: an **i** suffix marks the immigration and citizenship
versions (CELI Impatto i, CELI 1 i, CELI 2 i), and an **a** suffix marks the adolescent versions.
CELI certificates do not expire.

### Pass rule: GLOBAL within each of two blocks

CELI is **not** modular per skill. The individual parts A, B and C are **summed into a single
written score**, and the oral is scored separately. A candidate must clear **two** thresholds, one
for the written block and one for the oral block. A weak reading section can be carried by a strong
writing section, which is exactly the opposite of the CILS rule.

**CELI Impatto (A1)**

| Block | Composition | Max |
|-------|-------------|-----|
| Prova scritta | Part A comprensione della lettura (8 points, 25%), Part B produzione scritta (8 points, 25%) | 16 |
| Prova orale | scored 0 to 6 on competence scales, multiplied by 2.67 (50%) | 16 |
| Total | | 32 |

Pass: **at least 8 in the written block AND at least 8 in the oral block**, total 16 or above out of
32. Result is reported only as SUPERATO or NON SUPERATO, with no grade bands.

**CELI 1 (A2)**

| Block | Composition | Max |
|-------|-------------|-----|
| Prova scritta | Part A lettura (30 points, 25%), Part B produzione scritta (20 points, 15%), Part C ascolto (40 points, 30%) | 90 |
| Prova orale | scored 1 to 4 on competence scales, multiplied by 10 (30%) | 40 |
| Total | | 130 |

Pass: **at least 54 in the written block AND at least 25 in the oral block**, total 79 or above out
of 130. Reported as SUPERATO or NON SUPERATO.

**CELI 2 (B1)**

| Block | Composition | Max |
|-------|-------------|-----|
| Prova scritta | Part A comprensione della lettura (40 points, 25%), Part B produzione scritta (40 points, 25%), Part C comprensione dell'ascolto (40 points, 25%) | 120 |
| Prova orale | scored 0 to 20 on competence scales, multiplied by 2 (25%) | 40 |
| Total | | 160 |

Pass: **at least 72 in the written block AND at least 22 in the oral block**. The overall result is
then banded:

| Score | Grade |
|-------|-------|
| 138 to 160 | A, ottimo |
| 115 to 137 | B, buono |
| 94 to 114 | C, sufficiente |
| 60 to 93 | D, insufficiente |
| 0 to 59 | E, gravemente insufficiente |

The exam is passed with a total between **94 and 160**. Note that this means a B1 candidate faces
**three** numeric conditions: 72 written, 22 oral, and 94 overall. Clearing the two block minima
alone gives 94, exactly the band floor, so the three conditions are consistent rather than
contradictory.

Timing at CELI 2 (B1): 2 hours for the first booklet (reading plus writing), 20 minutes for
listening, about 12 minutes for the oral.

**CELI capitalizzazione.** A candidate who passes the written block but fails the oral, or the
reverse, may carry the passed block for **one year, meaning two exam sessions**, and re-sit only the
failed block. This is a two-way split, not a per-skill split as in CILS.

## CILS versus CELI at B1: which rule Corlang implements

The two bodies genuinely disagree in structure, so there is no single "Italian B1 pass rule".

- **CILS UNO-B1** is modular per skill: 5 abilities, 11 of 20 required in each, 55 of 100 total.
- **CELI 2 (B1)** is global within blocks: 72 of 120 written, 22 of 40 oral, 94 of 160 overall.

Corlang's B1 mock applies the **CILS per-ability rule**, since it is the stricter of the two: a
learner who reaches sufficiency in every skill separately also satisfies CELI's summed thresholds,
while the reverse is not true. The CILS B1 Cittadinanza rule (7 of 12 in each of 4 sections, 28 of
48, no carry-over) is implemented as the separate citizenship mock.

## A1 and A2 exams exist in both families

The Corlang course puts a mock at the end of every level, and both families supply a real template
at every level even though no Italian legal requirement sits below A2:

- **CILS A1** and **CILS A2** standard exams, 5 abilities, 60 points, 7 per ability, 35 total.
- **CILS A2 Integrazione**, the shortened 4-ability variant aimed at the integration agreement,
  48 points, 7 per ability, 28 total.
- **CELI Impatto (A1)** and **CELI 1 (A2)**, written plus oral blocks as tabulated above.

So the A1 and A2 Corlang mocks are anchored to published formats, not invented.

## Vocabulary reference

Italian has no single official per-level word list of the kind the Goethe-Institut publishes for
German. The two references that do the job:

**1. Nuovo vocabolario di base della lingua italiana (NVdB)**, Tullio De Mauro and Isabella Chiari,
2016, an update of De Mauro's 1980 *Vocabolario di base* built on a re-sampled corpus and on GRADIT
usage marking. Roughly **7,000 to 7,500 entries** in three bands:

| Band | Approximate size | What it is |
|------|-----------------|-----------|
| Vocabolario fondamentale | ~2,000 | the highest-frequency core, covering the bulk of any ordinary text |
| Alto uso | ~3,000 | frequent but below the core |
| Alta disponibilità | ~2,500 | words rarely written yet felt by speakers as immediately available (everyday objects, actions) |

This is the anchor for the Corlang Italian deck's level banding. It is a reference inventory, not
teaching material; Corlang authors its own sentences and never copies entries. Note that the NVdB
is banded by frequency and availability, **not** by CEFR level, so the mapping onto A1, A2 and B1 is
Corlang's own and must be documented as such.

**2. Profilo della lingua italiana: livelli di riferimento del QCER A1, A2, B1, B2**, Barbara
Spinelli and Francesca Parizzi, La Nuova Italia, 2010. This is the Italian **Reference Level
Description**, produced under the Council of Europe RLD programme in collaboration with the CVCL of
the Università per Stranieri di Perugia. It breaks each CEFR level into grammatical, lexical and
thematic inventories, and it is the closest thing Italian has to an official per-level content
specification. Use it for level banding decisions; use the NVdB for frequency cross-checks.

**Not verified:** a published per-level word count from *Profilo della lingua italiana* could not be
confirmed live today. Do not quote a per-level lexical size for Italian in app copy.
